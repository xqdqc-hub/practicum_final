01
Part One

Commercial Business Analysis

Mapping the existing retail and food ecosystem within walking distance of each proposed station to understand current commercial vitality along the corridor.

Methodology

Data & Approach

  • OpenStreetMap POIs — restaurants, cafes, bars, fast food, bakeries, and retail shops downloaded via OSMnx for the full corridor.
  • OpenData Philly Business Licenses — active food establishment licenses from the City, filtered to remove closed and expired records.
  • 10-minute walk sheds built from the actual OSM street network — not radius buffers — so only genuinely walkable areas are counted.
  • Spatial join assigns each business to its nearest station with no double-counting across overlapping sheds.
Sources: OpenStreetMap  +  OpenData Philly

02
Part Two

Real Estate Development Analysis

Identifying vacant parcels near each proposed station and their zoning — revealing where transit-oriented development could realistically occur.

Methodology

Data & Approach

  • Philadelphia OPA Dataset — 583,000+ parcels citywide with parcel geometry, zoning code, and assessed value.
  • Filtered to vacant land only — 42,387 parcels classified as Vacant Land (residential and non-residential) across the city.
  • Spatial join to 10-minute walk sheds assigns each parcel to its nearest station with no double-counting.
  • Zoning focus on development-potential zones: CMX (mixed-use), RM (multifamily), CA (commercial), and Industrial.
Source: Philadelphia OPA  (42,387 vacant parcels citywide)

Summary

Key Takeaways

🏪

Strong existing commercial base at inner stations

Broad-Erie, Bustleton, Rising Sun, and Cottman already have significant food and retail activity — transit would amplify an existing commercial ecosystem.

🏗️

Substantial vacant land for transit-oriented development

389 development-potential vacant parcels totaling ~61 acres sit within 10 minutes of a proposed station — a strong signal for developer interest.

📐

Outer stations show strong development potential

Stations like Adams and North 9th have large industrial parcels prime for mixed-use redevelopment, even with less current commercial activity nearby.

🗺️

Zoning is the key variable for developers

CMX and RM-zoned vacant parcels near transit are the highest-value opportunities — the interactive maps let stakeholders filter to exactly those sites.
